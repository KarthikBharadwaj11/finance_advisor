BUDGETING PRINCIPLES AND FRAMEWORKS

## The 50/30/20 Rule

The simplest evidence-based budgeting framework:
- 50% of after-tax income: Needs (rent/mortgage, utilities, groceries, minimum debt payments, insurance)
- 30% of after-tax income: Wants (dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, travel)
- 20% of after-tax income: Savings and debt repayment above minimums

Adjustments for high cost-of-living areas: Allow up to 60% for needs, reduce wants to 20%.
Adjustments for aggressive wealth building: Reduce wants to 15–20%, increase savings to 25–35%.

## Zero-Based Budgeting

Every dollar of income is assigned a purpose at the start of the month. Total income minus total allocated = zero. Benefits:
- Forces intentionality about every spending category
- Eliminates "lifestyle creep" — unconscious spending increases
- Reveals hidden expenses and subscriptions

Best for people with irregular income or significant debt payoff goals.

## Savings Rate: The Most Important Number

Your savings rate (savings / gross income) determines retirement timeline more than investment returns:
- 10% savings rate: ~43 years to retirement (from zero)
- 20% savings rate: ~37 years to retirement
- 30% savings rate: ~28 years to retirement
- 50% savings rate: ~17 years to retirement
- 70% savings rate: ~8 years to retirement

(Assumes 5% real return, 4% safe withdrawal rate in retirement)

The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement targets 25–33x annual expenses as the retirement number (4% withdrawal rule), achievable much faster with high savings rates.

## Housing Cost Benchmarks

Housing (rent/mortgage + utilities) should not exceed:
- 28% of gross monthly income: Front-end ratio (lenders' guideline)
- 36% of gross income: Total debt including housing (back-end ratio)
- Rent: Maximum 30% of gross income (traditional "rent burden" threshold)
- Mortgage: No more than 2.5–3x your annual gross income for the purchase price

## Priority Order for Financial Resources

1. Employer 401(k) match — free money, always capture first
2. High-interest debt payoff (above 6–7% APR)
3. Emergency fund (3–6 months expenses in cash)
4. Max out HSA (if available — triple tax advantage)
5. Max out Roth IRA ($7,000/year) or Traditional IRA
6. Max out 401(k) ($23,000/year)
7. 529 college savings (if applicable)
8. Taxable brokerage account for additional investing
9. Consider additional real estate or alternative investments

This ordering optimizes tax efficiency and risk management simultaneously.

## Lifestyle Inflation: The Wealth Killer

As income grows, expenses have a strong tendency to grow proportionally — this is lifestyle inflation. The remedy is the "50% rule": When income increases, allocate at least 50% of the raise to savings/investment. This preserves lifestyle improvements while accelerating wealth accumulation.

## Budget Categories and Benchmark Percentages

Typical monthly budget breakdown as % of take-home income:
- Housing: 25–35%
- Food (groceries + dining): 10–15%
- Transportation: 10–15%
- Healthcare: 5–10%
- Insurance: 5–8%
- Utilities + Phone + Internet: 5–8%
- Personal care: 3–5%
- Entertainment: 5–8%
- Savings + Investments: 15–25%
- Debt payments (beyond minimums): 5–15%

## Net Worth Tracking

Monthly net worth tracking is the single most motivating financial habit:
Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

Age-based net worth benchmarks (rough, median American data):
- Age 30: 0.5–1x annual income
- Age 40: 2–4x annual income
- Age 50: 5–8x annual income
- Age 60: 8–12x annual income

The "Millionaire Next Door" benchmark: Net worth should be approximately (Age × Annual Income) / 10

## Automating Good Financial Behavior

The most effective budgeting system is one that runs on autopilot:
1. Paycheck arrives → auto-transfer to retirement accounts (pre-tax)
2. Remainder in checking → auto-transfer savings percentage to savings/investment accounts
3. Remaining in checking = guilt-free spending money
4. Never manually move money to savings — automate it on payday

This "pay yourself first" system eliminates willpower dependency and ensures consistency.
